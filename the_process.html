Other Resources

Petition

In order to even get a visa, you must have a sponsor (either a US citizen/permanent resident family member, or prospective employer) who is willing to file a petition for you. They must send this petition to the USCIS. For the types of forms your sponsor must sign, click the "Other Resources" tab. Your Petition must be approved before you get a visa.


Petition Approval

Depending on which type of visa you applied for, it can take up to 1 year or more to get your visa approved. Visa approval can also be tough because of the limits which can be set on a certain type of visa or limits on immigrants from your country of origin. Depending on your circumstances, if your petition is approved, it's time to begin National Visa Center Processing (NVC). For more information, click the "Other Resources" tab.


Visas

At this point, it is highly reccommended to have an accessible email address and phone number which you will readily respond to. In this step, you must try to get a visa interview at a U.S. Embassy or Consulate overseas. In order to do so, you must first choose an agent to represent you. Your agent can be your sponsor or even yourself. You must formally select your agent by filling out the DS-261 Form. This process can take up to 3 weeks for your form to be approved. Click the "Other Resources" tab to learn more about the form.


Fees

After getting your DS-261 form approved, you must pay 2 online fees: Immigrant Visa Application Processing Fee and the Affidavit of Support Fee. It can take up to around 1 week for your payment to be processed. Click the "Other Resources" tab for more information. And now, you must submit your forms and documents to the NVC. This will be further explained in the next steps.


Submitting Forms and Documents

You must first complete the Application for Immigrant Visa and Alien Registration (Form DS-260). This form can be found in your online portal. You must submit it online and print it out for your visa interview. Next you must submit financial documents. You must submit an I-864 form (completed by your petitioner which proves that they have a steady income and can financially support you in the USA). Depending on what type of visa you have, you may need to sign a different form. Click the "Other Resources" tab to learn more about the form.

Supporting Documents

Now, you must collect any other supporting documents and submit it to the NVC. This inlcudes any civil documents necessary. To find out the specifics needed, click the "Other Resources" tab.

Submitting All Documents

After signing all these forms, they must be submitted to the NVC. You must submit all documents in one package. Please make sure you photocopy documents such as your birth certificate and passport. You must keep the real civil documents with you when you are getting interviewed. You can submit all your documents by your online portal, by mail, or by email. For more information, click the "Other Resources" tab.

The Interview & Post-Interview

Congratulations for making it this far! Now you must tackle the interview. The interview plays a huge role in your visa application. For more information, click the "Other Resources" tab. After the interview, if your visa is approved, your immigrant visa will be placed on your passport. You will recieve a sealed packet (which you should not open) which contains important documents for when you enter the USA. It is advised to travel to the USA before your visa expiration date and pay fees to the USCIS. Depending on your age/situation, you may be exempt from this fee. You may also be required to get vaccinated and get X-rays.

Entering the USA

After this entire process, you have to follow a few more steps to finally become a permanent USA citizen! For more information, click the "Other Resources" tab.
